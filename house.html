A 168 sq-ft. portable house built over the spring, summer, fall and winter of 2013. The frame is salvaged from a 1953 mobile home. Most of the materials are repurposed. The windows and doors are from a local landfill, and the aluminum roofing panels were found in a construction surplus pile on the side of the road. It is insulated with rigid polyiso from Repurposed Materials in Henderson, CO. The lumber is locally harvested beetlekill pine from NW Colorado. The project was built for a budget of less than $5000. The plans are rendered in Trimble SketchUp.
